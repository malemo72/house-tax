The concept of taxing property, including houses, has been around for centuries and varies depending on the region and the political system in place. The origins of house taxes can be traced back to ancient civilizations where rulers and governments levied taxes on property and land to fund public services, infrastructure, and governance. In modern times, the history of property taxes varies from country to country. For example, in the United States, property taxes have been in place since the colonial period, with some of the earliest instances of property taxation dating back to the 17th century.

Specifically, in the U.S., property taxes were levied by local governments to fund essential services such as schools, roads, and public safety. The exact dates and details of when property taxes began in each region within the U.S. would depend on local history and governance structures. Overall, the concept of taxing houses and property has evolved over time and is deeply intertwined with the development of taxation systems and governance structures in different societies throughout history.
